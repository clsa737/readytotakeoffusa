# Ready for Takeoff USA - Quick Start Guide

**Comprehensive Airline Interview Preparation System**

Welcome to Ready for Takeoff USA! This guide helps you navigate the complete program.

---

## Your 90-Day Journey

### **Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-3)**
**Goal:** Understand what airlines want and build your foundation

**Primary Work:**
- [ ] Module 1: Foundations (airline hiring process, CRM, career story)
- [ ] Complete CAPTAIN self-assessment
- [ ] Develop your 2-minute career story
- [ ] Begin researching target airlines

**Time Commitment:** 5-8 hours

**Instructor Check-in:** Review your career story

---

### **Phase 2: Behavioral Mastery (Weeks 4-6)**
**Goal:** Master storytelling and behavioral interview technique

**Primary Work:**
- [ ] Module 2: Behavioral Interview Mastery (storytelling, examples)
- [ ] STAR Method module (practice structural framework)
- [ ] Develop 7 core STAR stories (write them out)
- [ ] Practice each story 5+ times
- [ ] Record yourself and listen critically

**Time Commitment:** 10-15 hours

**Instructor Check-in:** Mock interview #1 (foundation)

---

### **Phase 3: Airline-Specific (Weeks 7-8)**
**Goal:** Become an expert on your target airlines

**Primary Work:**
- [ ] Module 3: Airline-Specific Preparation (Delta, United, American, Southwest)
- [ ] Research each target airline deeply
- [ ] Develop strong "Why [Airline]?" answers
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 thoughtful questions to ask
- [ ] Network with pilots at target airlines

**Time Commitment:** 8-10 hours

**Instructor Check-in:** "Why [Airline]?" answers feedback

---

### **Phase 4: Technical & Practical (Weeks 9-10)**
**Goal:** Master technical knowledge and professional presence

**Primary Work:**
- [ ] Module 4: Technical Interview & Practical Assessments (regulations, weather, fuel, systems)
- [ ] Module 5: Professional Presence & Application Materials (resume, appearance, networking)
- [ ] Study FAR Part 121 key sections
- [ ] Practice technical Q&A scenarios
- [ ] Refine resume and application materials

**Time Commitment:** 10-12 hours

**Instructor Check-in:** Mock interview #2 (behavioral deep-dive) + resume review

---

### **Phase 5: Final Preparation (Weeks 11-12)**
**Goal:** Ready for actual interviews

**Primary Work:**
- [ ] Module 6: Mock Interview Program (full simulations)
- [ ] Complete mock interview #3 (realistic full interview)
- [ ] Practice group interview dynamics (if applicable)
- [ ] Prepare interview day logistics
- [ ] Final review of all materials

**Time Commitment:** 8-10 hours

**Instructor Check-in:** Final readiness assessment

## Getting the Most from This Program

### **Study Tips**

**For Behavioral Material:**
- Read examples, then create YOUR version
- Practice speaking (not just reading)
- Record yourself and listen critically
- Get feedback from peers/mentors
- Refine continuously

**For Technical Material:**
- Understand concepts, not just facts
- Practice decision-making scenarios
- Discuss with experienced pilots
- Study regulations in context
- Connect to real-world experience

**For Professional Presence:**
- Video record yourself
- Get feedback on appearance and delivery
- Practice in front of mirrors
- Build confidence gradually
- Remember: professionalism is a skill

### **Time Management**

**Realistic commitment:**
- 8-10 hours per week for 12 weeks
- Some weeks more (application prep, mock interviews)
- Some weeks less (research, independent study)
- Total: ~100-120 hours for complete program

**Flexibility:**
- Adjust timeline based on your starting point
- Compress if you're already experienced
- Extend if you need more practice
- Continuous improvement even after getting job

## Common Questions

**Q: How long is this program?**
A: 90 days (12 weeks) is the recommended timeline, but you can move faster or slower based on your needs. Complete it at your pace.

**Q: Can I do this without an instructor?**
A: Modules 1-5 are self-directed. Module 6 (mock interviews) is best with feedback, but you can practice with peers.

**Q: What if I'm already experienced?**
A: You can move quickly through foundations and focus on modules relevant to your goals.

**Q: Is this only for international pilots?**
A: No! It's for all pilots seeking U.S. airline jobs: domestic, international, career changers, all backgrounds.

**Q: How do I get certified as an instructor?**
A: See CONTRIBUTING.md. We're building a community of experienced pilot mentors.
