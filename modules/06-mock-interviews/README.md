# Module 6: Mock Interview Program & Resources

## Overview

This module provides a complete mock interview program, evaluation rubrics, and instructor resources for conducting realistic practice interviews and providing meaningful feedback.

---

## Mock Interview Program Structure

### Why Mock Interviews Matter

- **Safe environment** to practice without stakes
- **Real feedback** on performance
- **Confidence building** before actual interviews
- **Identifying strengths** and development areas
- **Practicing delivery** of stories and answers

### Three-Interview Progression

**Interview 1: Foundation**
- Establish baseline performance
- Practice basic interview skills
- Identify major development areas
- Build confidence

**Interview 2: Development**
- Refine storytelling and delivery
- Address previous weaknesses
- Practice handling tough questions
- Develop composure

**Interview 3: Final Preparation**
- Simulate actual airline interview
- Demonstrate readiness
- Practice under pressure
- Final refinements

## Mock Interview Question Bank

### Behavioral Interview Questions

**Tell Me About Yourself (2-3 minutes)**
- "Tell me about yourself"
- "Walk me through your career to this point"
- "What attracted you to aviation?"

**Leadership & Decision-Making**
- "Tell me about a time you had to make a difficult decision"
- "Describe a situation where you had to lead a team"
- "Tell me about a time you had to convince others of your viewpoint"
- "Give an example of when you took initiative"
- "Tell me about a time you influenced someone's thinking"

**Teamwork & Collaboration**
- "Tell me about a time you worked with someone difficult"
- "Describe a time you supported a team member"
- "Tell me about your experience with CRM"
- "How do you contribute to a positive team environment?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to work outside your comfort zone"

**Adaptability & Resilience**
- "Tell me about a time you had to adapt quickly"
- "Describe a situation where things didn't go as planned"
- "Tell me about your worst day at work"
- "How do you handle frustration?"
- "Give an example of learning something new"

**Mistakes & Learning**
- "Tell me about a mistake you made and how you handled it"
- "Describe a time you failed"
- "Tell me about a situation where you didn't meet expectations"
- "How do you respond to criticism?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to admit you were wrong"

**Safety & Professionalism**
- "Tell me about a time you had to enforce a safety procedure"
- "Describe a situation where you had to report someone's unsafe behavior"
- "Tell me about a time you prioritized safety over schedule"
- "How do you ensure you're always current and proficient?"

**Motivation & Goals**
- "Why do you want to be an airline pilot?"
- "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
- "What are your career goals?"
- "Why are you interested in our airline specifically?"

### Technical Interview Questions

**Regulations & Procedures**
- "Tell me about FAR Part 121 requirements for dispatch"
- "When would you declare MINIMUM FUEL?"
- "What are the requirements for an alternate airport?"
- "Explain fuel planning requirements"
- "When is an alternate required?"

**Weather Decision-Making**
- "Describe your approach to wind shear"
- "How do you handle icing conditions?"
- "What would you do if you encountered severe turbulence?"
- "Tell me about thunderstorm avoidance"
- "How do you make weather go/no-go decisions?"

**Systems & Emergency**
- "You're at 35,000 feet and lose pressurization. What do you do?"
- "Engine failure after takeoff—walk me through it"
- "Describe a loss of electrical power scenario"
- "What's your approach to an engine fire?"
- "Tell me about go-around procedures"

**Operational Scenarios**
- "You're 45 minutes from destination with 50 minutes fuel and ATC requests a 30-minute hold. What do you do?"
- "Weather at your destination is below minimums and deteriorating. Alternate is 200 miles away. Your options?"
- "First officer seems fatigued before a flight. How do you handle?"
- "Passenger medical emergency at flight level 350. Your approach?"

### Airline-Specific Questions

**About Our Airline**
- "Why [Airline]?"
- "What do you know about our operations?"
- "What attracts you to [Airline]?"
- "Tell me about our fleet"
- "What do you know about our culture?"

- Greeting & Introduction (2 min)

## Feedback Delivery Guidelines for Instructors

### Effective Feedback Structure

**Start Positive:**
- Acknowledge effort and preparation
- Highlight specific strengths
- Show genuine appreciation

**Be Specific:**
- Reference specific moment/question
- Not: "You were nervous"
- Yes: "On the third question, I noticed you paused and took a breath before answering. That was excellent—it showed you were thinking carefully rather than rushing"

**Ask Before Telling:**
- "What do you think went well?"
- "How do you feel about that answer?"
- "What would you do differently?"
- Then offer your feedback

**Balance & Growth Mindset:**
- Two strengths to every development area
- Frame development areas as growth opportunities
- "You have strong foundations in X. To take it to the next level, work on Y"

**Actionable Recommendations:**
- Not: "You seemed unprepared"
- Yes: "You clearly prepared on regulations. Next time, also prepare 2-3 stories about international flying, since the airline emphasizes global operations"

### Sample Feedback Conversation

**Instructor:** "Let's talk about how that interview went. How are you feeling about it?"

**Pilot:** "I think I did okay, but I got nervous on that fuel question."

**Instructor:** "Tell me more about what happened with the fuel question."

**Pilot:** "I knew the answer, but I blanked for a second."

**Instructor:** "That pause actually worked in your favor. You took a breath, collected your thoughts, and gave a clear, accurate answer. That's exactly what professional pilots do—they pause, they think, they answer. That's a strength.

One thing I noticed: you didn't acknowledge the fuel situation until ATC called out MINIMUM FUEL. In reality, a professional captain calls it first. Next time, add that step to your decision-making framework. Also, you might add: 'I'd coordinate with dispatch before it gets critical.' That shows you're thinking like an airline captain, not just reacting.

Overall, that was solid. You're showing good technical knowledge and decision-making. Keep building your confidence in these technical answers."

## International Pilot Considerations

### Adapting Content for International Pilots

**Strengths to Emphasize:**
- International experience as asset
- CRM from diverse backgrounds
- Language skills
- Adaptability

**Areas to Address:**
- U.S. regulatory framework
- U.S. communication styles (direct, less hierarchical)
- U.S. airline culture differences
- Work authorization process

**Mock Interview Adjustments:**
- Frame international experience positively
- Address work visa/authorization questions directly
- Discuss cultural transition approach
- Emphasize adaptability and learning

### Sample Scenario Questions for International Pilots

1. "How does your background in [country/airline] prepare you for U.S. airline operations?"
2. "Tell me about a time you had to adapt to a different culture or communication style"
3. "What attracted you to pursue airline flying in the United States?"
4. "How do you approach learning new regulations and procedures?"
5. "Tell me about your understanding of CRM compared to your previous experience"

## Resources & References

### For Instructors

**Interview Facilitation:**
- Ask open-ended questions
- Let silence sit (don't fill pauses)
- Allow follow-up questions
- Take notes during interview
- Maintain neutral expression

**Video Recording:**
- Have candidate review self
- Ask what they'd do differently
- Discuss what went well
- Build confidence through self-reflection

**Mock Panel:**
- Vary interviewers when possible
- Include different perspectives
- Provide varied questions
- Simulate panel dynamics
