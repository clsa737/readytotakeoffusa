# CAPTAIN Framework
## Communication, Attitude, Professionalism, Teamwork, Adaptability, Integrity, Navigation

The **CAPTAIN Framework** is the comprehensive assessment model used throughout Ready for Takeoff USA to evaluate pilot competencies beyond technical flight skills.

---

## The Seven Pillars

### **C - COMMUNICATION**
The ability to express ideas clearly, listen actively, and adapt messaging for the audience.

#### In Interviews:
- Clear, professional speech without filler words ("um," "like")
- Active listening to questions
- Asking clarifying questions
- Professional vocabulary and grammar
- Appropriate tone and pace

#### In the Cockpit:
- CRM principles (call-outs, briefings, inquiries)
- Clear commands and feedback
- Reporting to ATC professionally
- Briefing crew on procedures and plans
- Asking questions when unclear

#### Development Questions:
- How do you ensure you've been understood?
- How do you handle communication with someone who has a different style?
- Tell me about a time miscommunication caused a problem. How did you fix it?

---

### **A - ATTITUDE**
Professional demeanor, respect, coachability, and positive orientation to challenges.

#### In Interviews:
- Respectful engagement with all interviewers
- Positive framing of challenges and setbacks
- Coachability and willingness to learn
- Enthusiasm for the role and airline
- Grace under pressure

#### In the Cockpit:
- Professional interaction with crew
- Safety-first mentality
- Willingness to follow procedures
- Positive response to feedback
- Commitment to continuous improvement

#### Development Questions:
- How do you respond to critical feedback?
- Tell me about a time your initial approach didn't work. How did you adjust?
- What does "safety culture" mean to you?
- How do you stay positive during challenging days?

---

### **P - PROFESSIONALISM**
Reliability, integrity, attention to detail, and adherence to standards.

#### In Interviews:
- Punctuality and preparation
- Appropriate professional appearance
- Organized and well-documented materials
- Honest and accurate information
- Respect for interview process and time

#### In the Cockpit:
- Following checklists and procedures
- Accurate logbook entries
- Maintenance and equipment care
- Regulatory compliance
- Professional standards in all situations

#### Development Questions:
- Describe your approach to precision and attention to detail.
- Tell me about a time you had to enforce a procedure or standard.
- How do you maintain professionalism in high-stress situations?
- What does integrity mean in aviation?

---

### **T - TEAMWORK**
Collaboration, supporting others, sharing information, and contributing to collective success.

#### In Interviews:
- Appropriate use of "we" vs. "I"
- Credit given to team members
- Recognition of others' contributions
- Collaborative problem-solving approach
- Positive references to working relationships

#### In the Cockpit:
- CRM implementation
- Supporting captain/first officer
- Briefing and debriefing
- Cross-checking procedures
- Asking for help when needed
- Supporting crew member workload

#### Development Questions:
- Tell me about a time you supported a team member who was struggling.
- Describe a situation where you had to speak up for safety.
- How do you contribute to a positive team environment?
- Tell me about your experience with CRM principles.

---

### **A - ADAPTABILITY**
Flexibility, resilience, quick thinking, and effectiveness in changing circumstances.

#### In Interviews:
- Flexible thinking when asked follow-up questions
- Composure when challenged on responses
- Willingness to discuss alternative approaches
- Recovery from mistakes or misspoken information
- Openness to diverse perspectives

#### In the Cockpit:
- Responding to weather changes
- Handling equipment malfunctions
- Changing plans and procedures
- Learning new aircraft/systems
- Working with different captains/crews
- Adjusting to operational changes

#### Development Questions:
- Tell me about a time you had to change your plan quickly.
- Describe your experience learning a new system or procedure.
- How do you handle uncertainty?
- Tell me about a time things didn't go as expected.

---

### **I - INTEGRITY**
Honesty, ethical decision-making, and alignment of actions with values.

#### In Interviews:
- Truthful and accurate resume/application
- Honest discussion of experience and qualifications
- Acknowledging limitations
- Consistent story across interviews
- Following interview guidelines

#### In the Cockpit:
- Reporting accurate flight data
- Disclosing safety concerns
- Following regulations even when inconvenient
- Honest self-assessment of capabilities
- Ethical decision-making under pressure
- Refusing unsafe actions

#### Development Questions:
- Tell me about a time you had to make a decision between what was easy and what was right.
- Describe a situation where you had to report something uncomfortable.
- How do you maintain your integrity under pressure?
- Tell me about a time you had to say "no" to a superior.

---

### **N - NAVIGATION**
Strategic thinking, forward planning, goal-setting, and understanding the bigger picture.

#### In Interviews:
- Clear understanding of career goals
- Knowledge of airline and industry
- Thoughtful questions about role and company
- Long-term vision and planning
- Understanding how position fits into career path

#### In the Cockpit:
- Situational awareness
- Route planning and briefing
- Anticipating potential issues
- Understanding company operations
- Professional development and currency
- Mentoring and knowledge sharing

#### Development Questions:
- Where do you see yourself in five years?
- What do you know about our airline and why you want to work here?
- How do you stay current and continue learning?
- Tell me about a time you anticipated a problem before it occurred.

---

## Using CAPTAIN in Interview Preparation

### Self-Assessment
Rate yourself 1-5 in each pillar:
- **1** = Significant development needed
- **3** = Solid foundation
- **5** = Exemplary strength

Identify your strongest and weakest areas. Develop specific STAR stories that demonstrate strength in weaker areas.

### Story Development
For each pillar, develop 1-2 strong STAR stories:

- **Communication**: Story about resolving a miscommunication
- **Attitude**: Story about responding to criticism or setback
- **Professionalism**: Story about attention to detail or integrity
- **Teamwork**: Story about supporting a colleague
- **Adaptability**: Story about changing plans or learning something new
- **Integrity**: Story about ethical decision-making
- **Navigation**: Story about planning or strategic thinking

### Interview Strategy
- Listen to the question carefully—identify which CAPTAIN pillar it's assessing
- Select a STAR story that demonstrates strength in that area
- Use CAPTAIN language in your response
- After interviews, debrief using CAPTAIN framework—which pillars did you demonstrate well?

## Instructor Notes

*The CAPTAIN framework provides a common language for discussing pilot competencies. Use it throughout the course:*
- *Debrief mock interviews against CAPTAIN pillars*
- *Have students assess themselves and identify development areas*
- *Discuss real incidents and discuss which CAPTAIN areas were involved*
- *Frame coaching feedback using CAPTAIN language*
- *Help international pilots understand how their background aligns with CAPTAIN principles*
- *Use CAPTAIN to connect behavioral questions, technical decisions, and professional expectations*

---

**Key Takeaway:** The CAPTAIN framework bridges interview preparation and professional excellence. Demonstrate these seven qualities and you'll succeed in interviews and thrive as an airline pilot.
