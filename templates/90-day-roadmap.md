# 90-Day Preparation Roadmap
## Your Path to Airline Interview Success

This roadmap provides a structured 12-week plan to prepare for airline interviews. Adapt the timeline based on your current position in the hiring process.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-3)

### Week 1: Self-Assessment & Career Story
**Goal:** Understand where you stand and build your narrative

- [ ] Complete CAPTAIN self-assessment (rate 1-5 in each pillar)
- [ ] Identify your strengths and development areas
- [ ] Develop your career story (2-3 minute version)
- [ ] Outline key life/professional experiences
- [ ] Practice telling your story to a friend or mentor
- [ ] **Instructor Check-in:** Review career story for authenticity and clarity

### Week 2: Understanding Airline Expectations
**Goal:** Know what airlines are looking for

- [ ] Read Module 1: Foundations
- [ ] Research 2-3 target airlines (culture, values, fleet)
- [ ] Understand CRM principles and how they apply
- [ ] Review airline pilot job descriptions
- [ ] Identify key competencies in job postings
- [ ] **Instructor Check-in:** Discuss airline research findings

### Week 3: Resume & Application Foundation
**Goal:** Prepare materials for application

- [ ] Review Resume Review module
- [ ] Update resume with professional formatting
- [ ] Ensure logbook is accurate and complete
- [ ] Gather documentation (certificates, ratings, medical)
- [ ] Draft professional bio/summary
- [ ] **Instructor Check-in:** Submit draft resume for feedback

---

## Phase 2: Behavioral Mastery (Weeks 4-6)

### Week 4: STAR Method & Story Development
**Goal:** Master behavioral interview technique

- [ ] Study STAR method thoroughly
- [ ] Develop 7 core STAR stories:
  - Leadership experience
  - Conflict resolution
  - Handling failure/mistake
  - High-pressure decision
  - Safety/professionalism
  - Adaptation/change
  - Exceeding expectations
- [ ] Write out each story in full
- [ ] Time each story (target: 2-3 minutes)

### Week 5: Story Refinement & Practice
**Goal:** Deliver STAR stories smoothly

- [ ] Practice each story until comfortable (5+ times each)
- [ ] Record yourself and listen critically
- [ ] Eliminate filler words ("um," "like," "you know")
- [ ] Practice varying your pace and emphasis
- [ ] Get feedback from instructor or peer
- [ ] Refine based on feedback
- [ ] **Instructor Check-in:** Present 3 STAR stories for feedback

### Week 6: Expanding Your Story Library
**Goal:** Prepare for variety of behavioral questions

- [ ] Develop 5-7 additional stories for:
  - Teamwork and collaboration
  - Learning from others
  - Dealing with difficult people
  - Prioritization and time management
  - Customer service/passenger focus
  - Innovation or improvement
  - Specific airline culture fit
- [ ] Ensure stories demonstrate CAPTAIN pillars
- [ ] Practice telling stories in different ways (short vs. detailed version)

---

## Phase 3: Airline-Specific Preparation (Weeks 7-8)

### Week 7: Airline Deep-Dive
**Goal:** Prepare tailored answers for target airlines

- [ ] Research each target airline deeply:
  - Fleet and operations
  - Recent news and developments
  - Pilot hiring and training programs
  - Company culture and values
  - Recent safety or operational issues
- [ ] Develop strong "Why [Airline]?" answers
- [ ] Understand crew base operations and lifestyle
- [ ] Research interview panel composition
- [ ] **Instructor Check-in:** Present "Why [Airline]?" answers

### Week 8: Technical Interview Preparation
**Goal:** Prepare for technical/scenario questions

- [ ] Review technical interview prep module
- [ ] Study airline procedures (alternates, weather, fuel)
- [ ] Review federal aviation regulations (FAR Part 121)
- [ ] Prepare for scenario-based questions
- [ ] Practice explaining technical decisions
- [ ] Study weather decision-making
- [ ] Review fuel management and planning

---

## Phase 4: Professional Presence & Simulation (Weeks 9-10)

### Week 9: Professional Appearance & Communication
**Goal:** Master non-verbal communication

- [ ] Review professional appearance module
- [ ] Prepare interview attire
- [ ] Practice handshake, eye contact, posture
- [ ] Record yourself on video
- [ ] Review verbal communication:
  - Clarity and pacing
  - Professional vocabulary
  - Confidence without arrogance
  - Graceful handling of tough questions
- [ ] **Instructor Check-in:** Video feedback session

### Week 10: Mock Interview Program
**Goal:** Practice in realistic conditions

- [ ] Participate in first mock interview (video recorded)
- [ ] Receive feedback on CAPTAIN framework
- [ ] Identify specific areas for improvement
- [ ] Make adjustments
- [ ] Participate in second mock interview
- [ ] Assess improvement
- [ ] Practice handling interview panel dynamics
- [ ] **Instructor Check-in:** Debrief mock interview performance

---

## Phase 5: Advanced Preparation (Weeks 11-12)

### Week 11: Group Interviews & Virtual Formats
**Goal:** Prepare for different interview formats

- [ ] Study group interview dynamics
- [ ] Practice standing out professionally
- [ ] Research airline group interview format (if applicable)
- [ ] Study virtual interview best practices
- [ ] Test your technology (camera, lighting, background)
- [ ] Practice on video platform
- [ ] Prepare your interview space
- [ ] **Instructor Check-in:** Virtual mock interview

### Week 12: Final Preparation & Interview Strategy
**Goal:** Be ready for actual interviews

- [ ] Final mock interview
- [ ] Review application materials one last time
- [ ] Prepare thoughtful questions to ask
- [ ] Plan logistics for any in-person interviews
- [ ] Prepare elevator pitch for networking
- [ ] Create interview day checklist
- [ ] Plan follow-up strategy
- [ ] **Final Instructor Check-in:** Readiness assessment

## Key Success Metrics

By Week 12, you should be able to:

✈️ Tell your career story confidently (2-3 min, no notes)

✈️ Deliver 7+ STAR stories smoothly and authentically

✈️ Answer "Why [Airline]?" compellingly for your targets

✈️ Handle technical questions with confidence

✈️ Maintain professional presence (posture, eye contact, speech)

✈️ Demonstrate all CAPTAIN pillars in conversation

✈️ Ask thoughtful questions about the airline and role

✈️ Handle unexpected or difficult questions gracefully

---

## Maintenance Mode (After Week 12)

Once you reach 12 weeks:
- Continue mock interviews monthly (or bi-weekly if interviewing)
- Stay current on airline news and operations
- Refresh STAR stories as needed
- Practice presentation skills
- Maintain fitness and appearance
- Keep logbooks updated
- Network actively
- Apply to positions strategically

---

## Instructor Notes

*This roadmap is flexible. Adjust based on:*
- *Individual starting point (some may need more time on basics)*
- *Timeline to actual interviews*
- *International pilot considerations (visa, work authorization)*
- *Specific airline requirements or known interview formats*
- *Group vs. individual coaching needs*

*Use weekly check-ins to identify where individuals need extra support. The CAPTAIN framework provides consistent language for feedback. Celebrate progress and maintain momentum.*
